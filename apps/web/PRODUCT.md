# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fitness and discipline communities — groups of people holding each other accountable for daily habits (gym, diet, reading, cold plunges, coding challenges, you name it). Users join via invite links, complete daily tasks with proof submissions, and compete on a shared leaderboard. Miss one task and you restart from Day 1.

Their context: they're on their phone mid-workout, or at their desk between meetings, or winding down at night logging the day's completed tasks. They want to get in, prove they did it, see where they stand, and get out. The app should feel like the friend who won't let you skip leg day — not the HR compliance module.

## Product Purpose

HOBBIT is a group accountability tracker for discipline and habit challenges. It exists to make quitting harder than doing the work. Users compete on shared leaderboards, submit photo/text proof of task completion, and build streaks that reset to zero the moment they miss. Success means groups are active daily, proof submissions flow in, and the "here to annoy you into great habits" promise delivers real behavioral change.

## Brand Personality

**Aggressive, playful, relentless.** In-your-face accountability with a smirk. The drill sergeant best friend who won't let you quit — but makes it fun enough that you keep coming back. Tough love, never mean-spirited. The tone is competitive, anti-fluff, and results-over-feelings. Think Duolingo's pushy owl crossed with a gym buddy who texts you at 6am asking where you are.

Three-word core: **Aggressive. Playful. Relentless.**

## Anti-references

- **Enterprise SaaS dashboards**: No complex bloated UIs with 50 widgets, no nested data tables, no "export to CSV" energy. This is fast, focused, and task-oriented.
- **Corporate wellness apps**: No pastel gradients, no zen/meditation vibes, no "your journey matters" platitudes. HOBBIT doesn't coddle.
- **Cartoonish over-gamification**: Gamified but grounded. Streaks and leaderboards motivate but real proof submissions keep it honest. No confetti cannons for brushing your teeth.

## Design Principles

1. **Relentless accountability**: The UI should push users, not coddle them. Leaderboards, streaks resetting to zero, and public proof submissions create natural pressure. Let the data drive the behavior — not walls of motivational copy.

2. **Speed over sprawl**: Get in, log your proof, see the leaderboard, get out. Every interaction should be fast and focused. No nested menus, no dashboard bloat, no friction.

3. **Playful aggression**: Tough love delivered with a smirk. Competitive but never mean-spirited. The mascot, the copy, and the micro-interactions should feel like a friend holding you accountable — not a surveillance system.

4. **Proof-first culture**: Every completion requires evidence. The UI should celebrate proof submissions and make them the center of the experience, not an afterthought.

5. **Gamified but grounded**: Streaks, leaderboards, XP, and tiers create healthy competition, but real proof submissions anchor everything in reality. Earned achievements, not vanity metrics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA** compliance baseline
- Color is not the sole indicator of state (use icons, text, and shape alongside color)
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected throughout — animations have reduced-motion fallbacks already in place
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
- Screen-reader-friendly labels and ARIA where needed
